# Lesson Planning Worksheet

You fill this in. Claude writes the full 16 pages of content from it.
Takes about 3 minutes per lesson. You never have to be the piano expert —
you just have to make product decisions.

---

## 8 questions, that's the whole worksheet

### 1. Which lesson number is this?
`L__2_` (e.g. L42)

### 2. What tier + act does it fit in?
- Tier: Beginner 
- Act: (which 5-lesson group inside the tier)

### 3. What's the ONE thing a student should walk away knowing?
One sentence, plain English. No piano jargon needed.
    High keys right, low keys left. 

### 4. What kind of lesson is it?
Pick ONE:
- [ ] Teach — introducing a brand new concept (16 pages)


### 5. What does the student need to already know?
lesson 1.

### 6. What's the vibe of this lesson?
Pick the ones that feel right:
gentle, still playful

### 7. Piece connection (optional)


### 8. Anything Cleffy should definitely say?
Any cool phrase, metaphor, or comparison you want in the lesson.
same conecpt, the piano is also like stairs

## Example: Here's Lesson 16 planned in 3 minutes

```
1. L16
2. Beginner, Act 4
3. "Steps are the smallest move in music — from a line to the very next space, or a space to the very next line."
4. Teach (16 pages)
5. Prereqs: [11, 12, 13, 14] — knows what a staff is, middle C, lines and spaces
6. Vibe: Gentle + Playful. First real "reading" lesson.
7. No specific piece — but it'll set up Hot Cross Buns in L20
8. Mention that steps are like "walking" vs leaps which are "hopping."
```

That's it. Claude writes the 1,100-word lesson from that.

---

## When you're stuck

**If you don't know the answer to #3** ("the one thing"): just tell Claude "I don't know what the main idea should be — help me pick." Claude will propose 2-3 options and you pick.

**If you don't know the prereqs for #5**: skip it. Claude will figure it out.

**If you don't know the piece connection for #7**: skip it. Claude will find a good one from the anchor-piece list if it fits.

**If you're scared you'll pick wrong on the vibe for #6**: don't overthink it. You can always say "actually make it more serious" after reading the draft. The draft is faster to fix than plan.

---

## The golden rule

You are not trying to be a piano teacher.
You are trying to be the kind of person who says:
> "This lesson is good. Ship it."
> or
> "This lesson is boring. Try again — make it shorter."

That's the whole job. The piano expertise lives in Claude's brain, not yours.
